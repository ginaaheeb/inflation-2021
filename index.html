How Fast Prices Have Risen In Each Region of the U.S.

By Gina Heeb

NEW YORK — While consumers across the U.S. have seen rapid price increases in recent months, the sticker shock has been particularly severe in some regions of the country.

A closely watched gauge of inflation, the consumer price index, rose 6.2% in October. That was the fastest nationwide pace in more than three decades, but inflation rates have been even higher in the Midwest and the South.

Prices rose more than 7.3% that month in the census region that encompasses Iowa, Kansas, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, North Dakota and South Dakota, according to a monthly release from the Bureau of Labor Statistics. In some of the southeastern states, the CPI increased at nearly the same rate.

“There has been an unexpected inflation shock in the U.S. during 2021,” said James Bullard, the president of the St. Louis Federal Reserve.
